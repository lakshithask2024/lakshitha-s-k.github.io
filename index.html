About Me

I fell in love with statistics.
Everything followed.

I chose data science because statistics felt like a superpower: the ability to find signal in noise, to make the invisible visible, to turn messy real-world data into decisions that matter. That curiosity has never left me.

Data engineering and ML are where that curiosity became craft. I gravitated toward data engineering because infrastructure is everything. A brilliant model is useless without a pipeline that can feed it reliably at scale. I love building the systems underneath the headline: the ETL jobs, the vector stores, the reproducible workflows that make research actually ship.

My work spans financial NLP, agentic LLM systems, and large-scale batch processing. Whether building a RAG platform for hedge fund analysts or backtesting macro signals with FinBERT, the thread is always the same: rigorous engineering, clean architecture, and results that hold up.
